My journey

Hi, I’m Elena. I am a (trainee) Full Stack Software Developer, and used to be a Project and Programme Manager in the arts and cultural sector.
I am currently a student at Northcoders , loving every second of it and looking forward to starting my first job in the industry.

I am a proudly adopted Mancunian (not least because it’s the best city for sourdough pizza, so I don’t feel too far from home), and have been contributing to its cultural offering by organising intimate gigs throughout the city as City Leader of Sofar Sounds Manchester, and as an active Trustee of the Manchester Jazz Festival.

I loved my job,and my life as events organiser, but I lacked the challenge and excitement of working in a field where you get to learn every day. During my career, I’ve always felt drawn to tech and digital innovation, which is how the word ‘Digital’ became part of my previous job titles (everyone wants a “techy” in the office, right?). And even though I have been very lucky to never become unemployed during the pandemic (and have become an expert in running webinars in the meantime), this break from ‘normal’ life has given me the opportunity to stop and think about my career objective, my talents and my interests.

I have now realised that life as a programmer is even better than I had ever imagined! I can write code with people from all backgrounds, have fun, be creative and hopefully, one day, help to change the world we live in for the better.

I have been told that I could make a tree talk, but you can’t go wrong with me if you want to have a chat about podcasts, DIY, theatre, vegan recipes, pizza (did I mention I love pizza?), fun travelling adventures, hiking, TV shows, and (last but not least), the environment and how to live a more eco-friendly life.
